The author proposes a transformer variant built around a **fixed, mathematically-derived sparse backbone** called the "lattice spine":

- Positions: 0, 2, 4, 10, 24, 58, 140, 338, 816, 1970, ...
- Generated by the recurrence:  
  **Sₙ = 2·Sₙ₋₁ + 2·Sₙ₋₂ + 2·Sₙ₋₃** (with S₀=0, S₁=2, S₂=4)

This sequence grows roughly like **≈ 2.414ⁿ** (root of the characteristic equation), faster than exponential in base 2 but slower than base 3 — a kind of "golden-ratio-like" discrete geometric progression.

The claim: this specific indexing pattern has deep algebraic structure (closure under certain operations like X = Z − Y, W = Y − X, etc.), and routing information hierarchically through these exact positions enables:
- Efficient long-range communication (logarithmic depth)
- Natural multi-token (horizon) prediction — especially at lattice-aligned offsets (+2, +4, +10, ...)
- Extremely fast inference via self-speculative decoding
